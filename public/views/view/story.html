The origin of skyscrapers

Mother tell me of the birds,
how they soar,
the summer of their flights

They soar because they know the wind,
They know his currents
and his secret heart

Is their song happy?
Ever joyful, song on the air
Do they seek to conquer?
Only themselves

And why do they stop short
of the city’s towering gates,
Is the height too great,
the air too thin?

They laugh over such a height,
but are mindful of their kindred’s
fate:

Here in the city, birds do not fly;
overfed, grounded by their greed,
They burrow into the sky
